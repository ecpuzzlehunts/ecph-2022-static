Solution: Rock Climbing
Answer: FUJI

Written by quatrevingtneuf

In this puzzle, solvers are presented with a series of cryptic crossword clues, along with an orthographic view of a 5x10 grid. Solvers should begin by solving the clues; the definitions in each clue are underlined in the table below.

Clue Answer Explanation
neglect to make time (6) FORGET FORGE (make) + T (time)
monster's overpowered: start to struggle following endless pattern (7) CYCLOPS OP + S(truggle) following CYCL(-e)
keep up with new lost language (7) TAGALOG TAG ALONG (keep up) where N (new) is lost
past reports of worker showed signs of fatigue (6) BEYOND hom. “bee yawned”
develop alliance to protect gemstone (4) OPAL hidden; develOP ALliance
spread out goals in two different directions (6) SPARSE PARS (goals) in S and E
destroy depot or weapon (7) TORPEDO anag. DEPOT OR
alternate acronym of the nhl member (6) COYOTE alternating letters in aCrOnYm Of ThE
tough guy embraces thankless outsiders (5) GUTSY GUY embraces TS (outside letters of thankless)
pause in a runner's workout, it's said (5) LAPSE hom. LAPS
as an example, chad's western music (7) COUNTRY ddef
backstrokes' pace? (4) STEP elision; PETS (strokes) reversed
to get down, hold back+shift→→↓ (7) DEPRESS REPRESS, R (right) becomes D (down)
cripple to deteriorate after a block (6) DAMAGE AGE after DAM
possibly enter audio port (3) KEY hom. “quay”

As they are, these clues do not fit into their rows; they all have too many letters. Solvers may notice that all the clues are given in only lowercase letters; even terms that should be capitalized like Chad and NHL are left in lowercase. This, along with “ascending and descending” in the flavourtext, suggest that we should consider lowercase letters with ascenders and descenders. Indeed, we notice that not only do all the words contain letters with ascenders and/or descenders, there are exactly ten letters in each triplet that don't have either. We should fill the grid by “ascending” when encountering a letter with an ascender, “descending” when encountering a letter with a descender, and moving along the row when the letter has neither. The final result looks like this:

Viewing only the raised cells, we can read our final answer directly off the grid: FUJI.

Author’s Notes

A great source of agony when constructing this puzzle was deciding whether or not "i" counts as a letter with an ascender. In my (probably wrong) opinion, the tittle doesn't qualify as an ascender for puzzle purposes, but just to be safe, I avoided the letter altogether. I look forward to your emails telling me that I'm wrong.
