Solution: Entering Competitions
Answer: MACRON

Written by quatrevingtneuf

In this puzzle, we are presented with what appears to be the Puzzle Hunt Calendar, plus a mystery grid at the bottom. Of course, there aren't actually this many puzzle hunts coming up next year (or will there be? we don't know yet!), so we may suspect that they clue something else.

Many puzzlehunting teams have traditions when it comes to selecting a team name. For us, we like to pick a name which fits the pattern E* C*. Indeed, just like our old team names, our hunt name, and this puzzle's title, all of the fictitious hunts clue two-word phrases of the form E* C*. An example is even given in the flavour text: the Puzzle Hunt Calendar is an Extensive Compendium.

Some of the clues may seem a little underspecified at first, but once we solve some of the clues, we find that the lengths of each E* C* pair do not repeat. This means that we can fill everything into the grid given at the bottom. The lightest corner represents the shortest answer, with length of the E word increasing rightward along the horizontal axis, and the length of C increasing downward along the vertical axis. Another thing to notice is the dates; none of the dates are more than 26 days apart. Converting the gaps between dates to letters via A1Z26, we get the message, "WE ONLY DO THE HUNTS IF IT'S A WEEKEND". The hunts are already conveniently arranged in rows of 5, so taking weekend hunts as 1 and the others as 0, we can use 5-bit binary reading left to right to get our final answer:

C\E 3 4 5 6 7

The final answer is MACRON.
