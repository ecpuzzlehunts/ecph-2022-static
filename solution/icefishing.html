Solution: Ice Fishing
Answer: NAUTICAL MILE

Written by quatrevingtneuf

The main gimmick behind this diagramless crossword is that each instance of the ice cube emoji represents a "hole" (a "closed counter" in typography) that we need to make. This includes letters such as a, b, and d, as well as numbers such as 4, 6, and 9. Characters with multiple counters, such as B or 8, are represented by multiple ice cube emojis. The numbers are a good way to confirm this mechanic. Note that for this puzzle, g is considered to have a single "hole", though some fonts render it as having two. Some clues are fairly easy to fill in, but others may require some guesswork. After "making holes" in all the clues, we should arrive at a solved grid that looks like this:

There are a couple suspicious clues in there: 31-Across and 60-Down suggest that something with gills with a length of at least 4 is hidden in the grid. Indeed, there are several words hidden diagonally in the grid that are one letter off of types of fish (these are highlighted in the grid above). These are listed below, in order of where the changed letter appears in the grid.
