Solution: Too Hot!
Answer: IGNIMBRITE

Written by chuttiekang

The puzzle consists of sets of one or two English phonemes represented in the International Phonetic Alphabet. The phoneme groups in each line are presented in "alphabetical order" by the form of the symbol, with Greek letters coming at the end. Note that here affricates and diphthongs count as one, and that transcriptions are in American English pronunciation. The phoneme sets in each line can be rearranged to make two words. For consistency, single phonemes are always at the end of a word. In each group, two crossword clues can be formed by taking one word from each line. The answers to these clues match the enumerations in parentheses.

Taking the end of the first clue answer and the start of the second clue answer gives the name of a gem, phonetically. This is hinted at in the flavortext (“they were said to carry some real gems in there”). Finally, indexing (using the numbers in brackets) into the gem names, again phonetically, sounds out the answer IGNIMBRITE.

Author’s Notes

First, apologies to the non-Americans; hopefully the transcriptions weren't too terrible.

This puzzle went through several changes during writing. The original presentation of the mixed clues was traditional anagrams (e.g. ACEEEFILLLRSTTTU for FLUTTER/CELESTIAL), and the original extraction was letter indexing into the gem names. Then I got the suggestion to lean more into the phonetics aspect (which was already present in the hidden gems). Extremely fortunately, the phonetic extraction formed after changing some indexes and reordering. Going from letters to IPA was trickier, and I ultimately decided on pairs of phonemes after realizing how difficult it would be if they were all separate.

Here are some clue pairs that didn't make it into the final puzzle:

  • helicopter colloquially/thunder partner
  • potato type or color/third-largest Nevada city
  • rhombus-shaped heraldic charge/artificial sweetener branded Equal
